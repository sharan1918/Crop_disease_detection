    🌽 Maize Diseases

    Common Rust

    A fungal disease caused by Puccinia sorghi that produces reddish-brown pustules on leaf surfaces, reducing photosynthesis and grain yield.

    Symptoms

    Small reddish-brown circular or elongated pustules on both leaf surfaces. Pustules release powdery rust-colored spores when rubbed. Severe infection leads to premature leaf drying.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Puccinia sorghi. Spreads through wind-borne spores. Favoured by moderate temperatures (15-25°C) and high humidity.

    Organic Options
    Neem Oil Extract (Azadirachtin)₹300/acre

    Every 7 days • 65% Effective

    Cow Urine (Jivamrutha) + Sour Buttermilk₹50/acre

    Every 5 days • 50% Effective

    Chemical Options
    Propiconazole 25% EC₹650/acre

    14-21 days (Spray 1ml/L) • 90% Effective

    Tebuconazole 25.9% EC₹800/acre

    Once per season • 95% Effective

    Estimated Cost

    ₹400 - ₹1200 per acre depending on severity

    WHAT TO DO
    • ✅ Spray fungicide at first sign of pustules
    • ✅ Use certified disease-free seeds
    • ✅ Monitor crops regularly during humid weather
    • ✅ Remove and destroy infected plant debris after harvest
    WHAT NOT TO DO
    • ❌ Do not ignore early symptoms
    • ❌ Do not over-irrigate during humid conditions
    • ❌ Do not plant susceptible varieties in rust-prone areas
    • ❌ Do not delay fungicide application
    Gray Leaf Spot

    A serious foliar disease caused by Cercospora zeae-maydis that creates rectangular lesions on leaves, significantly reducing yield in severe infections.

    Symptoms

    Rectangular gray to tan lesions running parallel to leaf veins. Lesions are 2-8 cm long and sharply defined. Lower leaves affected first, progressing upward. Severe cases cause complete leaf blight.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Cercospora zeae-maydis. Survives in infected crop residue. Spreads by wind and rain splash. Favoured by high humidity and temperatures of 22-30°C.

    Organic Options
    Bio-fungicide (Bacillus subtilis)₹350/acre

    Every 10 days • 75% Effective

    Neem seed kernel extract (NSKE 5%)₹100/acre

    Every 7 days • 65% Effective

    Chemical Options

    15 days • 85% Effective

    Azoxystrobin₹1200/acre

    At disease onset • 98% Effective

    Estimated Cost

    ₹500 - ₹2000 per acre

    WHAT TO DO
    • ✅ Rotate crops every season
    • ✅ Remove infected crop debris
    • ✅ Apply fungicide before tasseling stage
    • ✅ Choose resistant varieties for planting
    WHAT NOT TO DO
    • ❌ Do not plant maize after maize continuously
    • ❌ Do not leave crop residues on the field surface
    • ❌ Do not ignore lesions on lower leaves
    • ❌ Do not skip recommended fungicide schedules
    Northern Leaf Blight

    A destructive leaf disease caused by Exserohilum turcicum that produces large cigar-shaped lesions, leading to significant yield losses of up to 50%.

    Symptoms

    Large cigar-shaped, elliptical gray-green lesions (5-15 cm long). Lesions start on lower leaves and move upward. Severely infected leaves appear scorched. Dark spore production visible on lesion surfaces in humid conditions.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Exserohilum turcicum. Spores spread by wind and rain. Thrives in moderate temperatures (18-27°C) with heavy dew and frequent rainfall.

    • ❌ Do not ignore early signs on lower canopy
    Banded Leaf and Sheath Blight

    A serious disease caused by Rhizoctonia solani affecting leaves, sheaths, ears, and stalks with characteristic banded patterns.

    Symptoms

    Irregular, water-soaked lesions with alternating dark and light bands on leaves and sheaths. Disease starts from lower parts and progresses upward. Affected ears show poor grain filling.

    Cause

    Caused by the soil-borne fungus Rhizoctonia solani f. sp. sasakii. Favoured by high humidity (>80%), high temperatures (25-32°C), and dense plant populations.

    🌾 Wheat Diseases

    Brown Rust

    Also called Leaf Rust, caused by Puccinia triticina. It is the most common rust disease of wheat in India, causing yield losses of 10-30%.

    Symptoms

    Small, round to oval, orange-brown pustules scattered on the upper leaf surface. Pustules are smaller than stem rust and do not form in definite lines. Severely affected leaves turn yellow and dry prematurely.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Puccinia triticina (syn. P. recondita). Spreads through wind-borne uredospores. Favoured by temperatures of 15-22°C with intermittent rains or heavy dew.

    Organic Options
    Neem Oil Extract (Azadirachtin)₹300/acre

    Yellow Rust

    Also called Stripe Rust, caused by Puccinia striiformis. A devastating wheat disease in cooler regions of India, capable of causing 100% yield loss in susceptible varieties.

    Symptoms

    Yellow to orange-yellow pustules arranged in long stripes along the leaf veins. Pustules appear mainly on leaves but can also occur on leaf sheaths and glumes. Affected plants show stunting and shrivelled grains.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Puccinia striiformis f. sp. tritici. Requires cool temperatures (10-15°C) with high humidity. Spreads rapidly by wind over long distances.

    Septoria Leaf Blotch

    A major foliar disease caused by Zymoseptoria tritici (formerly Septoria tritici), affecting wheat leaves and reducing grain quality and yield.

    Symptoms

    Irregular tan to brown lesions with dark speckles (pycnidia) on leaves. Lesions may merge and cause large areas of leaf death. Lower leaves are affected first. Severe infection leads to premature senescence.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Zymoseptoria tritici. Spread by rain splash carrying spores. Favoured by wet, humid conditions (15-25°C). Survives on wheat stubble between seasons.

    🍅 Tomato Diseases

    Early Blight

    A common fungal disease caused by Alternaria solani, affecting leaves, stems, and fruits. Causes significant defoliation and yield reduction in tomatoes.

    Symptoms

    Dark brown to black circular spots with concentric rings (target-board pattern) on lower leaves first. Spots enlarge and coalesce causing leaf yellowing and drop. Stem lesions are dark and sunken. Fruit develops dark, leathery sunken spots near the stem end.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Alternaria solani. Survives in infected plant debris and soil. Spreads by wind, rain splash, and contaminated tools. Favoured by warm, humid conditions (24-29°C).

    Organic Options
    Trichoderma harzianum soil treatment₹200/acre

    Pre-sowing + Every 15 days • 70% Effective

    Copper-based botanical fungicides₹450/acre

    Every 10 days • 60% Effective

    Chemical Options
    Mancozeb 75% WP₹400/acre

    7-10 days (2.5g/L) • 85% Effective

    Metalaxyl + Mancozeb₹900/acre

    Critical stages only • 95% Effective

    Estimated Cost

    ₹800 - ₹2500 per acre (requires repeated spraying)

    WHAT TO DO
    • ✅ Remove lower infected leaves promptly
    • ✅ Use drip irrigation instead of overhead watering
    • ✅ Apply preventive fungicide before monsoon
    • ✅ Stake plants to improve air circulation
    WHAT NOT TO DO
    • ❌ Do not use overhead sprinkler irrigation
    • ❌ Do not plant tomatoes in the same field repeatedly
    • ❌ Do not work in wet fields to avoid spreading spores
    • ❌ Do not ignore early spots on lower leaves
    Late Blight

    A devastating disease caused by the oomycete Phytophthora infestans, capable of destroying entire tomato crops within days under favourable conditions.

    Symptoms

    Large, water-soaked, pale green to dark brown irregular lesions on leaves. White cottony mould growth on underside of leaves in humid conditions. Stems develop dark brown to black lesions. Fruits show large, firm, dark greasy-looking spots.

    Cause

    Caused by the oomycete Phytophthora infestans. Spreads rapidly through airborne sporangia. Thrives in cool (10-20°C), wet conditions with high humidity (>90%).

    Leaf Mould

    A fungal disease caused by Passalora fulva (formerly Cladosporium fulvum) commonly seen in greenhouse and polyhouse tomato cultivation.

    Symptoms

    Pale greenish-yellow spots on upper leaf surface. Olive-green to greyish-brown velvety mould growth on the underside of leaves. Infected leaves curl, wither, and drop. Severe cases affect fruit set and quality.

    Cause

    Caused by the fungus Passalora fulva. Thrives in high humidity (>85%) and moderate temperatures (20-25°C). Common in poorly ventilated greenhouses. Spores survive on crop debris and structures.

    Bacterial Spot

    A bacterial disease caused by Xanthomonas species that affects leaves, stems, and fruits — causing scabby spots and significant market value loss.

    Symptoms

    Small, dark, water-soaked spots on leaves that become angular and necrotic. Spots may have yellow halos. Raised, scab-like spots on green fruits that become brown and rough. Defoliation in severe cases.

    Cause

    Caused by Xanthomonas vesicatoria and related species. Spread by rain splash, contaminated seed, and infected transplants. Favoured by warm (24-30°C), wet conditions.
